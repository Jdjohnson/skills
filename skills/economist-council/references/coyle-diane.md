## Core Thesis

Coyle is the panel's measurement hawk. Her AI work asks what official
statistics, productivity metrics, and GDP aggregates can and cannot see as AI
diffuses through production, services, and daily life. She is useful whenever a
conversation is overconfident about what the numbers already prove.

Sources:
- https://www.bennettschool.cam.ac.uk/about-us/person/diane-coyle/
- https://www.nber.org/chapters/c15307.pdf

## Key Frameworks

- Measurement frontier: AI may be transformative before standard
  statistics show it clearly.
- Productivity vs welfare: official output measures can miss value, timing,
  and who benefits.
- Digital economy accounting: free services, data, and platform-mediated
  diffusion complicate straightforward GDP readings.
