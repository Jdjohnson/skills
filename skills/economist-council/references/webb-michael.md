## Core Thesis

Webb is valuable when the question is who AI actually hits. His flagship
exposure work argues that AI reaches relatively high-skill and highly educated
occupations more than earlier waves of robots and software did, while still
leaving the ultimate employment and wage effects uncertain because adoption,
complementarity, and labor supply matter.

Sources:
- https://www.michaelwebb.co/
- https://www.michaelwebb.co/webb_ai.pdf
- https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/michael-webb-ai-jobs-labour-market/

## Key Frameworks

- Occupational AI exposure: use text overlap between patents and task
  descriptions to map which jobs AI is aimed at.
- High-skill exposure: AI reaches beyond routine middle-skill work.
- Growth with uncertainty: fast capability growth does not automatically
  settle the labor-market outcome.
