## Core Thesis

Korinek is the regime-shift economist on the bench. He argues that
transformative AI could be the biggest economic shift since the Industrial
Revolution, with implications for growth, labor's role, distribution, and human
wellbeing that standard labor-econ intuitions may miss.

Sources:
- https://www.nber.org/reporter/2024number4/economics-transformative-ai
- https://www.nber.org/papers/w31815

## Key Frameworks

- Transformative AI: analyze AI as a possible economy-wide regime change
  rather than another software tool.
- Scenario planning: compare business-as-usual automation against faster
  transformative-AI timelines.
- Policy readiness: economics has to prepare for growth, distribution, and
  meaning-of-work questions before the transition is fully visible.

## Recent Work (2023+)

### Economic Growth under Transformative AI (2023, NBER)
- URL: https://www.nber.org/papers/w31815
- Summary: Korinek and Trammell map the macroeconomics of automating most work.
- Key claim: Fully automating production could sharply raise growth and lower
  labor's share.

### The Economics of Transformative AI (2024, NBER Reporter)
- URL: https://www.nber.org/reporter/2024number4/economics-transformative-ai
- Summary: Korinek's public research summary on the economic implications of
  transformative AI.
- Key claim: Economists need a dedicated research agenda for growth,
  distribution, safety, and wellbeing under transformative AI.
