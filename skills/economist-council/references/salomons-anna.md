## Core Thesis

Salomons sits in the labor-market adaptation lane. Her work tracks how
technology changes skill demand, inequality, and employment, and her recent
public and research work emphasizes that adaptation depends on firms, task
demand, and how education and training systems update worker expertise.

## Key Frameworks

- Race between education and technology: worker outcomes depend on whether
  skill formation keeps up with technological change.
- Within-occupation skill updating: curricula and training content change
  alongside technology.
- Employment and inequality: technology effects have to be traced through
  wages, job structure, and institutional adaptation.

## Recent Work (2023+)

### Expertise at Work: New Technologies, New Skills, and Worker Impacts (2024, discussion paper)
- URL: https://madoc.bib.uni-mannheim.de/67832/1/dp24044.pdf
- Summary: Salomons and coauthors study how new digital technology changes
  vocational curricula and worker outcomes.
- Key claim: Educational updates help workers adapt to new demands for their
  expertise and can improve wages relative to outdated skill profiles.

### Anna Salomons receives Role Models in Economics Award (2024, Utrecht University)

- Summary: Utrecht summary of Salomons' technology-adoption and labor-market
  research agenda.

  and institutions, not a law of nature.
