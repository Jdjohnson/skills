## Core Thesis

Frey brings the historical-displacement lane to this panel. His recent AI work
argues that generative AI expands exposure well beyond the low-skill jobs that
anchored the original automation debate, while still insisting that long-run
outcomes depend on institutions, adaptation, and who captures the gains.

Sources:
- https://www.oxfordmartin.ox.ac.uk/downloads/academic/2023-FoW-Working-Paper-Generative-AI-and-the-Future-of-Work-A-Reappraisal-combined.pdf
- https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/people/profiles/carl-benedikt-frey/

## Key Frameworks

- Future of work through historical analogy: read AI against earlier
  industrial and computing transitions.
- Exposure reappraisal: generative AI reaches farther into cognitive and
  creative work than earlier machine-learning expectations suggested.
- Technology trap: distributional outcomes hinge on institutions, power,
  and who adapts fastest.
