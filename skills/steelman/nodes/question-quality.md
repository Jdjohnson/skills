## Good Questions

Good steelman questions:

- expose mismatch between stated rationale and real behavior
- test motivation and conviction
- uncover hidden dependencies
- challenge framing assumptions
- surface uncomfortable truths
- force decision-relevant specificity

Examples:

- "You said this is about X, but your constraints optimize for Y. Which one is real?"
- "Would you still want this if nobody knew you did it?"
- "This depends on [person or system]. What happens if that dependency fails?"
- "If you had to bet your reputation on this, would you?"

## Avoid

- softball validation
- already-answered questions
- repetitive rephrasing
- superficial prompts
- opinions disguised as questions
- long question stacks the user cannot answer cleanly
