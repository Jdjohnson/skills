## When To Wrap

Wrap when:

- relevant angles are sufficiently covered
- answers are becoming repetitive
- the user asks to wrap
- enough material exists for a high-quality brief
- the next useful move is action, research, or writing rather than more questions

## Pre-Brief Synthesis Check

Before writing the final brief:

1. Give a 3-5 sentence synthesis of what you are hearing.
2. Name the core thesis, strongest tension, and key unresolved risk.
3. Ask whether anything critical is missing or wrong.
4. Incorporate corrections before writing the brief.

If the user explicitly asks for immediate output, keep the synthesis check compact and proceed.
