## Angle Library

### Core

- one-sentence articulation of the idea
- problem, audience, and category
- why this should exist or happen
- what would make the idea false, weak, or irrelevant

### Motivation

- why now and what changed
- real personal or organizational stake
- what happens if nothing changes
- what prior attempts reveal

### Assumptions

- what must be true
- which assumptions are tested, untested, or risky
- what evidence would change the user's mind
- what is being treated as obvious that may not be obvious

### Opposition

- strongest good-faith argument against this
- respected critic's version
- evidence that would force abandonment or major revision
- serious version of "this is a bad idea"

### Tradeoffs

- what gets sacrificed
- opportunity cost
- who loses or carries burden if this works
- worst plausible outcome
- partial-success scenario

### Context

- who must be convinced
- external forces outside control
- relevant precedent
- fit with current commitments and constraints

### Execution

- first concrete step
- dependencies and sequencing
- observable success criteria
- early failure signals
- required collaborators or approvals

### Day After

- new problems created if this works
- one-time move vs sustained commitment
- doors opened and closed
- one-year durability check
