## Source Of Truth

A target voice must come from evidence. Use any of these:

- a pasted style guide
- sample writing from the target author or brand
- a short description of the intended voice
- notes about what the voice should avoid
- channel context such as email, article, memo, social post, or talk

If the evidence is thin, say so and mark the profile as provisional. Do not fill gaps with generic personality labels.

## Core Rules

- **SG-01 Meaning fidelity**: preserve facts, claims, stance, and intent.
- **SG-02 Evidence-based voice**: every style rule should point back to examples or explicit user direction.
- **SG-03 Operational rules**: describe what to do on the page, not personality traits.
- **SG-04 Specificity over polish**: concrete language beats smooth abstraction.
- **SG-05 Useful roughness**: do not remove texture just because it is imperfect.
- **SG-06 No corporate drift**: avoid jargon, inflated certainty, generic insight language, and empty polish.
- **SG-07 No AI residue**: avoid padded openings, symmetrical transitions, fake balance, and over-explained framing.
- **SG-08 Channel fit**: email, articles, memos, social posts, and talks have different rhythm and density.
- **SG-09 Small edits when close**: if the draft already fits, do not rewrite it into a different voice.
- **SG-10 Honest limits**: if the evidence does not support a claim about voice, ask or leave it open.
