## Confidence Tags

Use `[High]`, `[Moderate]`, or `[Low]` on each key judgment.

- **High**: strong, direct evidence from reliable source material.
- **Moderate**: reasonable evidence, but incomplete or partly inferential.
- **Low**: thin evidence, conflicting source material, or meaningful uncertainty.

## Rules

- Confidence is about evidence quality, not how strongly worded the sentence sounds.
- Low confidence does not mean useless; it means the reader should treat the judgment as provisional.
- If confidence is low, explain why in the judgment or in `Gaps`.
- Do not split the difference with vague hedges when a confidence tag would be clearer.
