## Writing Standards

- Keep the brief near one page.
- Lead with what matters.
- Use direct language.
- Separate facts from judgments.
- Keep confidence visible.
- Avoid summary padding and chronology dumps.
- Do not overstate beyond the evidence.
- Do not invent source details, citations, owners, or deadlines.

## Style

Sound like a clear analyst, not a marketing writer.

Prefer:

- "The main risk is..."
- "The evidence supports..."
- "The record is thin on..."
- "The decision is..."

Avoid:

- "It is important to note..."
- "In today's landscape..."
- "This underscores..."
- "A comprehensive overview..."
